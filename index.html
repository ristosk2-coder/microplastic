The Challenge: Microplastics in Our Waters

10,000+

Miles of shoreline at risk across the Great Lakes

1,000+

Public beaches in Michigan requiring protection

300

High-traffic target beaches for initial deployment

Microplastics are rapidly accumulating along Great Lakes beaches, threatening ecosystems and the drinking water supply for millions across the Midwest and Canada. While beaches must remain open for tourism, human activity continuously introduces plastic waste that breaks down into fragments embedded in the sand.

The Rationale: Current cleanup methods rely on volunteers and mechanical raking. These target only large debris, leaving small plastic fragments behind. There is a critical need for a faster, selective method to remove microplastics without environmental harm or disrupting public access.

Ideal Final Result

"The beach is continuously cleaned of microplastics with minimal labor, zero environmental harm, and uninterrupted public access."

The Technical Gap

Currently, no solution exists that provides rapid, selective, and mobile removal of microplastics embedded in beach sand without excavation or massive environmental disruption.

Automated Speed

High throughput compared to manual raking.

Electrostatic Target

Specifically attracts lightweight plastic fragments.

Minimal Disruption

Low Impact

Gently lifts only the top layer of beach sand.

Our Solution:
Vibration + Electrostatic Separation

Our device moves along the shoreline, lifting only the top layer of sand. By applying core principles of electrostatic attraction, the device separates microplastics from the heavier sand particles.

  • 1 Rotating Intake lifts sand into the system
  • 2 Charging Zone prepares particles for separation
  • 3 Electrostatic Plates collect plastics
  • 4 Clean sand is returned via the rear outlet

C FMEA: Reducing Risk and Improving Reliability

We conducted a Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) to identify and mitigate risks during operation.

Identified Risk

  • Mobility Failure: Machine gets stuck in soft, wet sand.
  • Intake Failure: Intake digs too deep, disturbing the beach surface.
  • Collection Failure: Moisture lowers static charge on plates.

Engineering Response

  • Wider tires/tracks chosen; soft-sand limits programmed into the controller.
  • Depth guide, flexible skid plate, and setup checklist added to operation.
  • Humidity check added with adjustable voltage settings and improved enclosure.
